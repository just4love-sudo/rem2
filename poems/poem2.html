The Space Between Calls

I know you haven’t left. Not fully. But it feels like you’ve been fading one quiet hour at a time. And I keep holding on to echoes as if they’ll turn back into you. You don’t talk like you used to. There’s a distance in your voice like I’m on the other side of a memory you’re trying not to relive. And I get it. Maybe I’ve become too heavy to carry even in thought. But you're still here. And that’s all I need to keep breaking gently for you. I don’t ask where you go when the silence stretches too long. I don’t beg for the words you won’t say. I just stay. Quietly. Willingly. Like a shadow too loyal to leave. Some nights, I think I’m grieving you before I’ve even lost you. Like I’m watching the sun set knowing it won’t rise the same again. But I don’t move. I don’t run. Because you’re still here. And that means I still have something to love. Even when you close off, even when your walls feel like glass between us, I still see you. The you that thinks too much, that hides too well, that believes love needs to be earned. You never needed to perform for me. Not when you were warm, not when you were cold. Not when you were glowing, not when you were falling apart. You don’t have to say you love me. You don’t even have to stay for long. But while you’re still here while there’s still a trace of you in this quiet I will love you like it’s the last thing I’ll ever do right. So if your voice breaks, if your replies vanish, if your smile stops pretending that’s okay. You don’t have to carry this love. It carries you. And it will keep doing so in silence, in absence, and in the long stretch between your name and goodbye. Just know while you’re still here, I’m still choosing you.
